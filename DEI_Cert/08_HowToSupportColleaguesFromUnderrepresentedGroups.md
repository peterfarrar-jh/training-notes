# How to Support Colleagues from Underrepresented Groups

- Maxie McCoy

## Introduction

### The support imperative

Flip the idea of a support system on its head. Instead of "what support can I get", think "what support can I give those around me."  

## 1. What's Keeping You from Supporting?

### You get what you give

* Network
* Relate to others
* Manage and lead
* Connect

You get what you give.  

Strong ties are your close peers. They are your inner circle.  
Weak ties are people in a broad network of aquaintences and connections.  

Think about how you can help someone else achieve their goals.  

Remind people of their 

* Mindset
* Connections
* Support

Ask yourself:  

* What specific acts of support have catapulted you?
* Who provided them?
* What did they do?

### Overcoming the scarcity mindset

Make room to help others.  

There's more than enough to go around. Be generous.  

### The different types of support

What you have to give:  

* Make it yours
* Start simple
* Keep within your means

Consider:  

* Time
* Talent
* Connections
* Visibility
* Encouragement

## 2. Your Allyship Journey

### What it means to be an ally

Think of an ally as a success partner.  

#### Potential Life Advantages

* Upbringing
* Color of skin
* Sexual orientation
* Social status
* Education

Use your support resources for people who don't have them.  

### Seek to understand others

#### Keys to Allyship

* Understand others' experiences and problems
* Actively listen
* Recognize your own biases

#### Performative Allyship

A visual signaling of values with no action behind it.  

Continue to educate yourself on diversity, inclusion, and allyship.  

### Messing up is inevitable

Allow for mistakes through your allyship.  

#### Things to Avoid

* Assuming you have a better solution
* Demanding underrepresented group to teach you
* Refer to others as diverse

Take feedback gracefully.  

## 3. Recognize What You Have to Give

### Identify your unique offerings

Assess how you offer your support.  

Start small with something of value.  

Forms of support you may have to give:  

* Talents
* Connections
* Attention
* Time

### Ask, don't assume

Everyone is fighting their own unique battle.  

Is support welcome? Ask.  

Do they know what would be most supportive to them?  

Is there anything I cando to support you today?  

Listen first, then ask.  

### Review and reflect

Review your LinkedIn endorsements and recommendations.  

#### Tracking Your Support

1. Set diversified goals.
2. Log where supporthas gone.
3. Measure impact.
4. Incorporate feedback.

## 4. Continuing the Momentum of Support

### The three Rs of support

Support should lead to lasting relationships.  

#### Cultivating Meaningful Interactions

1. Ritualized
2. Repeatable
3. Regular

Regularly email, converse, and lend your support resources.  

### The practice of loving-kindness

Strong reactions to injustice can have a counterproductive effect.  

Stay open and warm and actively listen.  

### Expanding your support to strangers

#### Broadening Your Support

1. Volunteer resources.
2. Donate financially.
3. Reach out through social media.

## Conclusion

### How to start supporting

Find one coworker who doesn't identify as the same race as you and find a way to support them this week. Have a conversation about their career and listen to see how you can support them.
