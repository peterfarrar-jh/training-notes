## 2. Why Allyship Matters

### The link between inclusion, equity, and allyship

**Imclusion:**  
Creates an environment where people feel expected, reflected, and respected.  

**Equity:**  
Reveals unequal opportunities and where work needs to be done.  

**Allyship:**  
Removes barriers, examines systems, and brings us to equity.  

### Allyship and institutional power in the workplace

Allyship creates inclusive environments where employees feel engaged.  

Allyship challenges group think.  

People are attracted to companies working on allyship.  

Think about barriers that can be removed.  

## 3. Allyship Tools and Practices for Leaders

### Allyship is the responsibility of leaders

Leaders are responsible for creating the envionment...  

Make decisions through the lens of inclusion.  

How can you use your privilege to positively impact a particular group?  

Create pathways for success.  

Explore how bias can impact decisions.  

Hold conversations.  

Listen to lived experiences.  

Do your research.  

Speak out and share your learnings.  

Build relationships.  

### How allies behave in the workplace

Listen and learn.  

Build relationships.  

Amplify voices. (first listen learn and build relationships)

Follow the lead of those you're supporting.  

Interrupt the bias that you see.  

### How being an ally shapes your industry and community

How are different groups impacted by your community and industry?  

* What does this policy mean for this group?
* In what ways will they be disadvantaged?

Talk to people in your organization about inequities.  

## 4. What Makes You an Ally?

### Misconceptions about allyship

Misconception 1:  
You're doing something for this group.  

Allyship is about connectivity.  

Misconception 2:  
People jump in right away.  

Allyship starts with you.  

Misconception 3:  
Allyship is an intellectual exercise.  

Allyship is taking action and risks.  

Ally is not a title that is taken. It's a title that is earned.  

Allyship is a journey.  

### Centering, acceptance, and allyship

Progress requires allies in positions of power to take action.  

Take the time to understand how bestto support groups.  

Believe and lean into the stories of those with lived experiences.  

Give people space.  

When an ask is made, act on it.  

### Leading like an ally

* Give grace to yourself and others.
* Stay focused on the journey.
* What legacy are you leaving?
