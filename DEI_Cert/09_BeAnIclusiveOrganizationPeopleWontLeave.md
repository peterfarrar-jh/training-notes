# Be an Inclusive Organization People Won't Leave

* Jodi-Ann Burey

## Introduction

### Be an inclusive organization people won't leave

Revolving door of talent is costly and adds to burnout for the remaining employees. Why do people quit?  

## 1. Embrace Authenticity for All

### Help all employees bring their authentic selves

Why is leaving one's authentic self so intuitive for underrepresented employees?  

* Underrepresentation
* Microaggressions

#### Creating an Authentic Environment

1. Take ownership of creating an inclusive workplace culture.
2. Understand the experiences underrepresented professionals face at work.
3. Identify core barriers within your organization.

### Be authentic in your policies and practices

#### Closing the Authenticity Gap

1. Mind your authenticity shortcuts
   * Meaningfully increase the diversity of your workforce.
   * Have varying levels of seniority.
   * Ensure underrepresented employees have a voice.
   * Give room for underrepresented employees to decline opportunities to represent you.
2. Admit you know more than you think.
   * Utilze available resources.
   * Conduct listening tours.
   * Bring your ideas to the discussion.
   * Stay open and listen to others.
3. Audit your practices against your goals.

### Be authentic in your policies and practices

Customers believe that businesses can influence social change and expect them to take action.  

#### Making an Authentic Impact

1. Evaluate your level of investment
2. Identify your key issues and build on your fluency
3. Take a multi-pronged approach to your advocacy
4. Communicate the "why" to your workforce

## 2. Cultivate True Belinging to Retain Talent

### End imposter syndrome

**Imposter Syndrome:** Experience of lacking an internal sense of your own success and worthienss, despite achievements and accolades that prove otherwise.  

#### Ending Imposter Syndrome

* Pay attention to employee's experience
* Look for scalable and effective solutions
* Use existing data sources
* Set up accouintability mechanisms

### Manage conflict with integrity across differences

* Understand your own conflict style.
* See whether your conflict style is creating pitfalls in your conflict resolution policies.
* Change your mindset.
* Acknowledge power dynamics.

## 3. Embrasing Total Wellness at Work

### Physical wellness

* Reach beyond compliance.
* Don't be afraid to try something new.
* Ensure great policies are great for **everyone**.

Face the reality with **inclusion** at the core.  

### Financial wellness

* Ensure your data is complete and accurate.
* Conduct a pay equity audit.
* Create transparent policies.

End policies that prevent employees from talking about compesation packages.  
Stop asking prospective hires for pay history.  
Adopt pay transparency policies.  

### Intellectual wellness

What gets in the way of the intellectual and professional growth or employees?  

* Acknowledge and address how the organization might be suppressing growth and influence.
* Ensure underrepresented employees have equal access to influenctial sponsors and networking with key influencers.
* Prioritize flexibility and accessibility to support inclusion.
